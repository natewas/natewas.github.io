AI-Powered Assessor

TL;DR: Building an AI grading assistant to evaluate short-answer responses from Certified Admin exams—reducing grading time by 50% while maintaining a 95–99% accuracy match with human graders. The tool aims to provide consistent, contextual feedback to learners, handle complex inputs, and scale human effort across seven exams and 70+ unique questions.

To improve the efficiency and consistency of grading Certified Admin exams, I’m building an AI-powered tool that evaluates short-answer responses across 70+ questions from seven exams. The goal is to reduce manual grading time by 50% while maintaining a 95–99% agreement rate with human graders. The tool uses MC-GPT trained on course materials, pre-scored responses, and company-specific context to deliver accurate, context-aware assessments. It also generates clear feedback for examinees—something that’s currently limited due to time constraints.

This system is designed to handle a variety of real-world scenarios: vague or overly detailed responses, nuanced industry-specific answers, and learners with different levels of experience. It also avoids common AI pitfalls by not learning from incorrect or partially correct historical data. By running AI evaluations alongside human graders and comparing results, we’re able to refine the tool’s accuracy and reliability before expanding its use. Success is being measured through grading speed, accuracy, feedback quality, and overall user satisfaction from both graders and learners.

The project is moving through multiple phases: prototyping in MC-GPT, pilot testing with blind comparisons, application development for scaled grading, and eventual integration with Skilljar (pending feasibility). We’re also evaluating the broader implications of AI in education, including ethical considerations and potential expansion to other training areas. If successful, this tool will not only streamline grading but also elevate the overall certification experience for global learners.
